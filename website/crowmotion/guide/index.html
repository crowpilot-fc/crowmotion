Setup guide

From parts to first flight.

This guide takes a built CrowMotion tracker all the way to a camera that follows your head: configure the tracker, connect your radio, mix the trainer channels, and plug in the two servos. It covers firmware v0.1.2.

1. How it all fits together

Your head becomes two trainer channels: pan streams on TR1 and tilt on TR2 (both changeable in the config UI). Everything after the radio is ordinary RC plumbing - you mix the trainer channels onto two output channels, and the servos plug into those receiver slots.

2. What you need, built and flashed

This guide starts where the product page ends. If you have not yet: the parts list and wiring (pick your radio in the calculator to see exactly what to buy), the browser flasher, and the pan/tilt mounts and servos for the aircraft side. Come back here with a flashed tracker.

3. Configure the tracker
1

Open the config hotspot

With the radio disconnected, tap the tracker four times. Join the WiFi network crowmotion-XXXX (default password crowmotion) and open 192.168.4.1.

2

Teach it its mounting

Mount the tracker on your goggles first, then run Auto-detect in the Orientation tab while wearing them level. Any mounting position works - the tracker learns which way is up.

3

Note your channels

In the Channels tab, pan defaults to TR1 and tilt to TR2. Leave them unless they clash with another trainer setup. Watch the Live tab to confirm the 3D head follows you.

4

Close the hotspot

Quad-tap again to close config mode. Bluetooth is paused while the hotspot is open, so the radio can only connect after this step. The LED returns to a slow heartbeat: that means advertising, ready to pair.

4. Connect your radio

Path A - FrSky Bluetooth trainer

On EthOS radios (developed against the X20S), the Bluetooth trainer lives in the radio's Bluetooth settings: enable trainer mode and pick the crowmotion-XXXX device. The tracker's LED goes solid when connected, and the radio's trainer inputs start moving with your head.

5. Mix the trainer channels

This is the step most first-time setups get stuck on. The trainer inputs (TR1, TR2) do nothing until your model's mixer routes them to output channels. Pick two channels your model does not use for flying - CH6 and CH7 are typical on a simple fixed wing.

1

TR1 to a free channel

In the model's mixer, add a mix on CH6 with source TR1 (pan). Full weight, no expo.

2

TR2 to the next one

Same again on CH7 with source TR2 (tilt).

3

Put it on a switch

Gate both mixes with a spare switch so you can turn head control on and off in flight. Off should hold the camera at center.

SCREENSHOT: mixer page, TR1 on CH6 and TR2 on CH7

Menu wording differs between EthOS and EdgeTX, but the shape is the same everywhere: trainer input as a mix source on a free output channel.

6. The aircraft side
1

Plug in the servos

Pan servo into the receiver slot for CH6, tilt servo into CH7 - the same channels you mixed. Make sure your receiver or BEC can feed two extra 9g servos.

2

Center before assembly

Power everything up, look straight ahead, double-tap to recenter, and only then fit the servo horns and camera so the mount points forward at center.

3

Check the directions

Look left - the camera should look left. Look up - camera up. If an axis is reversed, flip Invert for that channel in the config UI's Channels tab. No re-gluing, no mixer surgery.

Movement too twitchy or too shallow? Tune sensitivity, deadband, and output range per axis in the Response tab, live, while wearing the goggles.

7. Preflight checks and troubleshooting
Before every flight
Double-tap recenters the camera with your head level and straight ahead.
Your head-control switch turns tracking on and off, and off holds center.
Power the tracker off: the camera should return to center, not freeze off-axis.
If something is off
The radio cannot find the tracker Bluetooth is paused while the config hotspot is open - quad-tap to close it. The LED should show a slow heartbeat (advertising).
Quad-tap does nothing The hotspot only opens while the radio is disconnected. Disconnect or power off the radio first.
Axes swapped or reversed Re-run Auto-detect in Orientation with the tracker mounted, then use Invert per channel for anything still backwards.
The camera slowly creeps Yaw drift is normal without a magnetometer. Hold still for a few seconds so auto-calibration catches up, and double-tap to recenter any time.
Servos buzz or twitch at center Raise the deadband a little in the Response tab, and check the servos have solid power from the receiver or BEC.
LED blinks twice, repeatedly The IMU was not found - recheck the four wires and the AD0-to-GND jumper against the wiring table.
