How it works
1

Wear it

CrowMotion sits on your goggles and measures head pan and tilt with a 6-axis IMU, fused at 100 Hz.

2

It talks to your radio

It connects to your radio as a wireless trainer, over Bluetooth or through the CrowLink dongle.

3

The camera follows

Your radio forwards the channels, and two servos on the aircraft pan and tilt the FPV camera to match your head.

Works with almost any radio

No Bluetooth? No problem.

Your radio does not need Bluetooth. CrowLink is a tiny dongle you build yourself for about $8 (₹800). It plugs into the 3.5mm trainer jack and talks to CrowMotion wirelessly. If your radio has a trainer port, CrowMotion works.

Does it work with my radio?

The $5 (₹500) build works with radios that have the FrSky Bluetooth (PARA) trainer. Any other radio works too, with CrowLink.

Direct over Bluetooth - about $5 (₹500)

FrSky Tandem X20, X20S, X20 Pro, X18, XE (EthOS)
FrSky Taranis X-Lite Pro
FrSky Taranis X9D Plus 2019
FrSky Horus X10 / X10S, Taranis QX7 (with the FrSky Bluetooth module)

Verified on the FrSky X20S (EthOS). The others use the same FrSky PARA Bluetooth trainer and should work - if you have tested one, let us know.

Any other radio - add CrowLink

RadioMaster, Jumper, Flysky, and EdgeTX radios without the FrSky Bluetooth module connect through the DIY CrowLink dongle over the trainer jack, about $8 (₹800) more.

Why CrowMotion
$5

About $5 (₹500)

An ESP32-C3 Super Mini and an MPU6500 module. That is the whole tracker.

No wires to your radio

The link to your radio is fully wireless, and tap gestures replace buttons. Power comes from any USB-C power bank; a plug-in battery module is planned.

Self-calibrating

Continuous gyro auto-calibration kills yaw drift while you fly. Double-tap the tracker to recenter at any time.

Configure from your phone

Quad-tap opens a WiFi hotspot with a full web UI: live 3D head view, channels, response curves, and updates.

Updates over the air

Check and install new firmware from the config UI, or reflash from the browser. No cables, no toolchain.

Open source

Apache-2.0, built from scratch on ESP-IDF. Firmware, protocol, and hardware notes all public.

Flash it

Flash from this browser

Plug your board into your computer over USB-C and click Connect. The right firmware is picked automatically: an ESP32-C3 gets CrowMotion, an ESP32-C6 gets CrowLink. Works in Chrome and Edge on desktop.

This browser cannot flash over USB. Use Chrome or Edge on a desktop computer.

Board not showing up? Use a data-capable USB-C cable, hold BOOT while plugging in, and close any serial monitors.

Or open the standalone flasher at updates.crowpilot.in

Questions
Which radios work?

FrSky radios with the PARA wireless trainer (developed against an X20S on EthOS) connect directly over Bluetooth. For everything else, CrowLink plugs into the radio's trainer jack.

Has it flown?

The link is bench-verified against a real radio. Flight testing is in progress - this is an actively developed project, and the roadmap is public on GitHub.

Does it drift?

Yaw drifts slowly on any magnetometer-free tracker. Continuous auto-calibration removes most of it, and a double-tap recenters instantly.

How is it powered?

Over USB-C. Most builders run a short cable to a small power bank in a pocket or clipped to the goggle strap. A plug-in battery module is planned.

Is it laggy?

Head motion is fused at 100 Hz on the tracker and streamed to the radio at 50 Hz, the same rate a wired trainer cable delivers.

Is my radio's trainer mode safe?

Yes, that is the best part: flip the trainer switch and your radio sticks take over instantly, exactly like an instructor override.

Where do I get help?

GitHub Issues for bugs, GitHub Discussions for everything else.
